Autoprint Inc.

Co-Founder · Chief Mechanical Engineer

Co-founded a startup to build the Autoprint One — a universal device that lets any 3D printer start printing in seconds. Led product development, filed a provisional patent, and raised $11,183 on Kickstarter with 125 backers.

$11K Raised on Kickstarter
125 Backers
90% Setup Time Reduced

The Challenge

3D printing technology remained inaccessible to most people due to complex setup processes, printer-specific requirements, and lengthy configuration times that deterred potential users.

The Solution

Developed the Autoprint One - a universal device compatible with most existing 3D printers that automated setup and enabled users to start printing in seconds instead of hours.

Campaign Outcome

Despite raising over $11,000 and gaining 125 supporters, the campaign ultimately fell short of its $30,000 funding goal. Without sufficient capital to move into production, we made the difficult decision to close Autoprint Inc. This experience provided valuable insights into startup challenges, market validation, and the realities of hardware development.
