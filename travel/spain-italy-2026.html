Destinations
Train Schiphol → Centraal: 15 min. Tap any credit card at the gate — no ticket purchase needed. ~€11 each way.
⚠ Critical: Return to Schiphol airport at least 2.5 hours before the Madrid flight. Time this carefully.
Layover Highlights
  • Anne Frank House — book timed entry at annefrank.org (2 months ahead). Canal boat tours depart right outside (~€25).
  • Jordaan neighborhood — best photography: narrow canals, houseboats, leaning canal houses
  • Dam Square + Royal Palace — 15 min walk from Centraal station
🚆 Schiphol ↔ Centraal: 15 min, tap credit card, ~€11 each way. Trains run every 10–15 min.
July heat: 35°C+. Outdoors before 11am or after 7pm. Museums are perfect for the midday heat. Metro €1.50/ride.
Day 1 — Evening Arrival
  • Gran Vía + Puerta del Sol — evening walk, great energy
  • Tapas in La Latina: Calle de Cava Baja is the street to know
  • Dinner 9–10 PM (Madrid eats late — lean into it)
Day 2 — Full Day
  • Royal Palace — book ahead, allow 2–3 hrs, get the audio guide
  • Lunch at Mercado de San Miguel
  • Reina Sofía Museum — Guernica is on floors 2 and 4. Unmissable.
  • Retiro Park at golden hour
  • Mirador Madrid rooftop bar — evening drinks with skyline views
Day 3 Morning — Before Granada
  • Churros at Chocolatería San Ginés (open since 1894, open 24 hrs)
  • Bus or train to Granada
🚌 To Granada: ALSA bus ~€10–15 (1.5 hrs) or Renfe train. Both depart from central Madrid.
⚠ Stay in Granada, not Málaga. Book Alhambra tickets immediately — Nasrid Palaces sell out months ahead. Bring passport to enter the complex.
Granada specialty: free tapas with every single drink ordered. Eat and drink very well for almost nothing.
Day 1
  • Explore the Albaicín — Granada's ancient Moorish quarter, winding white-washed streets
  • Sunset at Mirador de San Nicolás — the iconic framed view of the Alhambra
  • Dinner in Albaicín — order a drink, receive a free tapa, repeat indefinitely
Day 2 — Alhambra Day
  • Full Alhambra morning: Nasrid Palaces + Generalife Gardens + Alcazaba fortress. Allow 4+ hours. Book at alhambra-patronato.es
  • Afternoon: Sacromonte — cave houses carved into the hillside above the city
  • Evening: Flamenco show — several venues in Sacromonte and Albaicín
✈️ Next: Train or bus from Granada to Málaga airport for the Ryanair flight to Naples (Jul 7–8).
Base in Sorrento — perfect hub connecting Naples, Pompeii, and the Amalfi Coast by rail and ferry.
Day 1 — Arrival + Pompeii
  • Naples airport → Pompeii Scavi via Circumvesuviana train (~40 min, €3)
  • Book skip-the-line tickets in advance. Arrive at opening. Allow 3–4 hours.
  • Same Circumvesuviana line continues to Sorrento (~40 more min) — check in, rest up
Day 2 — Amalfi Coast
  • Boat tour or ferry hopping: Positano → Amalfi town → swim in the Mediterranean
  • SITA bus between towns ~€2.50 / ferries €8–15 per leg
  • Positano: pastel cliffside houses, narrow staircased lanes
  • Amalfi: cathedral, paper museum, lemon everything
🚂 To Rome: Ferry Amalfi → Naples, then Italo or Trenitalia high-speed train (70 min, ~€20–40). Book ahead — prices rise fast.
⚠ Flight departs FCO 7:30 AM on Jul 13. Be at FCO by 5:30 AM.
Leonardo Express: Termini → FCO = 32 min, €14. First train ~5:23 AM.
Be at Termini station by 5:00 AM. Set two alarms.
Day 1
  • Colosseum + Roman Forum — book 9am timed entry at coopculture.it. Allow 3–4 hours.
  • Afternoon: Trastevere — cobblestones, ivy-covered walls, excellent trattorias
  • Evening: Piazza Navona + Campo de' Fiori
Day 2
  • Vatican Museums + Sistine Chapel — book at museivaticani.va. Skip the 2–3 hr standby queue.
  • Afternoon: Pantheon (free entry) + Spanish Steps + gelato
  • Evening drinks at a rooftop bar in the Prati neighborhood
Day 3 — Departure
  • Alarm at 4:30 AM
  • Walk to Roma Termini, depart 5:00 AM
  • Leonardo Express to FCO: departs ~5:23 AM, arrives ~5:55 AM — €14, buy night before
  • Check in at FCO by 5:30 AM (Aer Lingus closes check-in early)
