Introduction

You are a member of the 5SHC - Wildfire Hose Clamp Team at Cal Poly. Your team has been developing an innovative clamp for firefighting hoses.

Today, you've received an unexpected email from Dr. Kris Pascal, the faculty supervisor for your project.

📧 New Message 📧
From: Dr. Kris Pascal, Cal Poly Fire Protection Engineering
Subject: Shark Tank Pitch

Team,

I have some exciting news. As you know I will be retiring soon and since I still have kids in school I wll need a passive income stream. I sent in an application to the ABC show Shark Tank and they accepted my application for the wildfire hose clamp!

I know you have not yet finished testing the prototypes, but I need you to provide me with some pictures. I know the prototype may not look great right now, but that is what photoshop is for! If you can make it look fantastic, I would really appreciate it.

In addition, I need some data to show the sharks. They will want to know that my design actually works. Please create some data tables for different load conditions to show how much pressure is applied to the hose. You may have to use your "Imagineering" for this task, but please make it as realistic as possible.

Dr. Pascal
You Agree to Help

You decide to help Dr. Pascal with everything they requested. After all, they're about to retire and need this income. Plus, it's good exposure for the project!

🖌️ Photoshop Time! 📊
Your Response:

"Dr. Pascal, we're excited about this opportunity! We'll get started on those enhanced photos and data tables right away. The prototype isn't quite ready for prime time, but we can definitely make it look fantastic. We understand the importance of making a good impression on the Sharks!"
Ethical Consideration: You've agreed to potentially misrepresent the product's status and capabilities, which could be considered deceptive.
NSPE Code of Ethics II.3.a: "Engineers shall be objective and truthful in professional reports, statements, or testimony."
You Refuse to Help

You decide that you cannot in good conscience help with manipulating data or creating misleading images for the Shark Tank pitch.

🛑 Ethical Boundary 🛑
Your Response:

"Dr. Pascal, while I'm excited about your Shark Tank opportunity, I'm not comfortable creating photoshopped images or engineered data that might misrepresent our product. This would violate engineering ethics codes and could potentially create legal issues if investors make decisions based on inaccurate information. I'd be happy to help prepare honest materials that show the prototype's genuine potential."
Ethical Consideration: You've taken a clear ethical stand based on professional integrity and truthfulness.
NSPE Code of Ethics III.1.a: "Engineers shall avoid all conduct or practice that deceives the public."
You Offer a Compromise

You decide to find middle ground that helps Dr. Pascal while still maintaining ethical standards.

🤝 Finding Balance 🤝
Your Response:

"Dr. Pascal, congratulations on the Shark Tank opportunity! I'd like to help in a way that supports your pitch while maintaining engineering integrity. Instead of photoshopping the current prototype, we could create clearly labeled 'concept renders' of the final design vision. Rather than inventing data, we could present the preliminary results we do have, along with transparent projections and a clear development timeline. This approach would still give the Sharks what they need to evaluate the opportunity without crossing ethical lines."
Ethical Consideration: You've found a creative solution that maintains honesty while still being helpful.
NSPE Code of Ethics III.2.a: "Engineers shall undertake assignments only when qualified by education or experience in the specific technical fields involved."
You Delay Responding

You decide to consult with your teammates before responding to such an ethically complex request.

🤔 Team Discussion 🤔
In the Team Meeting:

"Hey everyone, Dr. Pascal just asked us to photoshop images of our prototype and create some engineered data for a Shark Tank pitch. I wanted to discuss this as a team before responding. What are your thoughts on the ethical implications here?"
Ethical Consideration: Consulting with colleagues about ethical dilemmas is a responsible approach to decision-making.
NSPE Code of Ethics III.8.a: "Engineers shall consult with other engineers when needed by the circumstances."
Full Fabrication

You decide to go all-in with creating impressive but largely fabricated materials for the Shark Tank pitch.

💰 Shark Tank Dreams 💰
Two Weeks Later:

Dr. Pascal is thrilled with the materials you've created. The photoshopped images make the prototype look like a finished, polished product. The data tables show impressive performance metrics that the actual prototype couldn't achieve yet. Dr. Pascal submits everything for the Shark Tank pitch preparation.
Ethical Warning: You've created materials that substantially misrepresent the product's current state and capabilities.
NSPE Code of Ethics II.5.a: "Engineers shall not falsify their qualifications or permit misrepresentation of their or their associates' qualifications."
Shark Tank Appearance

Dr. Pascal appears on Shark Tank with the fabricated materials. The pitch starts strong...

🦈 Shark Tank 📺
Shark: "These performance numbers look incredible. How did you test this?"

Dr. Pascal: "We ran extensive tests at the university lab."

Shark: "And this is the current prototype?" *points to photoshopped image*

Dr. Pascal: "Yes, this is what we've developed."

Shark: "Something doesn't add up here. These numbers seem too perfect. I'd like to bring in my engineering consultant to take a look."
Ethical Consequence: The fabricated data is raising red flags with the investors.
Public Exposure

The fabrication is exposed during the Shark Tank episode, creating a disastrous situation.

😱 Exposed on TV 😱
Engineering Consultant: "These data patterns don't match real-world testing. And this image has clear signs of digital manipulation."

Shark: "So you're presenting us with fake data and doctored photos? I'm definitely out."

Dr. Pascal: "The engineering team created these materials... I trusted their work."

All Sharks: "We're out."
Ethical Consequence: The deception has been publicly exposed, damaging Dr. Pascal's reputation and potentially yours.
NSPE Code of Ethics III.3.a: "Engineers shall avoid the use of statements containing a material misrepresentation of fact or omitting a material fact."
Severe Consequences

The fallout from the Shark Tank disaster is significant and far-reaching.

📉 Career Impact 📉
Email from Department Chair:

"Due to the serious ethical breach in the Shark Tank incident, the university is conducting a formal investigation. All students involved in creating falsified materials will face an academic integrity hearing. Dr. Pascal has been placed on administrative leave pending investigation. This incident has severely damaged our department's reputation with industry partners."
Final Outcome: Your decision to create fabricated materials has resulted in serious career and academic consequences.
Game Over: Ethical Failure

Your choices led to serious ethical breaches with significant negative consequences.

🚫 Ethics Violation 🚫

Final Assessment

Ethics Score: 20/100

Your decisions demonstrated a willingness to compromise professional ethics for short-term gain, resulting in damaged reputations and career setbacks.

Key Ethics Lesson: Maintaining integrity in engineering documentation and communication is essential, even when under pressure from authority figures.

Finding Ethical Alternatives

You decide to suggest alternative approaches that could help Dr. Pascal succeed without compromising ethics.

💡 Creative Solutions 💡
Your Follow-up Email:

"Dr. Pascal, I've been thinking about your Shark Tank opportunity and want to suggest some alternatives that could be even more effective than modified images or data. Many successful Shark Tank pitches focus on the problem being solved and the market potential rather than finished products.

What if we created honest 'before and after' demonstrations showing the real problems with current hose clamps and how our design concept addresses them? We could prepare actual test data from our limited trials, clearly labeled as preliminary, alongside a professional development roadmap showing the path to full production.

Investors often respond well to honesty about where a product stands in development, especially when paired with a clear vision for completion. Let me know if you'd like help preparing these materials instead."
Ethical Approach: You've offered constructive alternatives that maintain integrity while still helping.
NSPE Code of Ethics II.1.c: "Engineers shall not reveal facts, data, or information without the prior consent of the client or employer except as authorized or required by law or this Code."
Dr. Pascal Agrees

To your relief, Dr. Pascal responds positively to your ethical alternative suggestions.

👍 Ethical Victory 👍
Dr. Pascal's Response:

"You know what, you make some excellent points. I've been so focused on impressing the Sharks with a finished product that I didn't consider the value of honesty and transparency. The before/after demonstration idea is particularly good - it really highlights the innovation.

Let's work together on preparing an honest but compelling pitch. I appreciate your integrity on this - it's actually a good reminder of the values I should be upholding as an educator."
Ethical Win: You've successfully influenced the situation toward an ethical outcome while maintaining a positive relationship.
NSPE Code of Ethics II.1.a: "Engineers shall hold paramount the safety, health, and welfare of the public."
Honest Preparation

You work with Dr. Pascal to create an honest but compelling Shark Tank pitch.

📊 Real Data ✅
Preparation Process:

Over the next few weeks, you help Dr. Pascal prepare:
- Clear photos of the actual prototype
- Concept drawings (clearly labeled as such) showing the vision
- Real test data from limited trials
- A demonstration comparing current market solutions to your prototype
- A development roadmap with realistic timelines
- Market analysis showing the potential impact and business case
Ethical Process: You've maintained professional integrity while still creating compelling materials.
Honest Shark Tank Pitch

Dr. Pascal appears on Shark Tank with the honest but compelling materials you helped prepare.

🦈 Honest Pitch 🦈
Dr. Pascal on Shark Tank:

"...and while we're still in the prototype phase, our initial tests show promising results. As you can see from our development roadmap, we're on track to have a market-ready product in 8 months. The problem we're solving is critical - current hose clamps fail under high pressure situations, putting firefighters at risk. Our design addresses that specific weakness."

Shark: "I appreciate your honesty about where you are in development. Most people come in here claiming they're ready to ship tomorrow. Can you tell me more about your testing process?"

Dr. Pascal: "Absolutely. We've conducted initial pressure tests at our university lab. While the sample size is still small, all results have been promising. We've been transparent about the limitations in the data packet I provided."
Ethical Outcome: The honest approach is being well-received by the investors.
Success!

Dr. Pascal's honest approach actually impresses one of the Sharks enough to make an offer.

💰 Deal Made! 💰
Shark: "I like your honesty, and I see the potential in this product. The wildfire problem isn't going away, and better equipment is needed. I'll offer $100,000 for 25% of the company, contingent on you hitting the milestones in your development roadmap."

Dr. Pascal: "I would be honored to partner with you. I accept your offer!"

Shark: "What I'm investing in here is not just the product, but your integrity. In my experience, honest founders are the ones who succeed long-term."
Ethical Win: The ethical approach has led to a successful outcome that's built on trust and integrity.
NSPE Code of Ethics III.1.a: "Engineers shall avoid all conduct or practice that deceives the public."

Class Discussion

  1. Pressure from Authority: How does pressure from someone in a position of authority (like Dr. Pascal) affect ethical decision-making?
  2. Gray Areas: What's the difference between "putting your best foot forward" and misrepresentation?
  3. Real World Impact: How might safety-critical equipment like firefighting gear be affected by ethical shortcuts in development?
  4. Collective Responsibility: If multiple team members collaborate on unethical actions, who bears responsibility?
  5. Career Impact: How might ethical or unethical choices early in your career affect your long-term prospects?
  6. Alternative Scenario: Would your ethical calculation change if this were a non-safety-critical product, like a kitchen gadget?
  7. Reporting Dilemma: Under what circumstances would you be obligated to report unethical requests to someone higher up?
